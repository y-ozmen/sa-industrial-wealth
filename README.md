# San Antonio Industrial Wealth & Strategic AI Advisor

## Overview
This repository hosts a dynamic Single Page Application (SPA) designed to map the macroeconomic and structural impact of Toyota's historic $3.6 billion "Project Orca" expansion in San Antonio, Texas. 

Standard press releases focus on capital. This interactive dashboard evaluates the fundamental shift from labor-intensive assembly to high-complexity automation arbitrage, identifying specific bottlenecks in regional human capital and supply chain readiness.

## Core Analytical Pillars
1. **Investment Scaling:** Visualizing the density of the Texas-Mexico Automotive Supercluster (TMASC) and the massive capital upgrades required by Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers to meet strict JIT/JIS protocols.
2. **The Educational Pull-Model:** Mapping the region's rapid-credentialing infrastructure, specifically highlighting the newly launched **Alamo Technical Institute (ATI)** as the critical solution to the "middle-talent" deficit for electro-mechanical technicians.
3. **Scientific R&D Core:** Correlating the immediate need for advanced R&D in Tribology and AI Knowledge Engineering (driven by the SwRI AFEV consortium and UTSA CAMLS) to support hybrid EV drivetrains.

## The "Pracademic" Talent Gap
The data identifies an acute structural deficit at the apex of the engineering hierarchy. The dashboard visualizes the urgent market need for leadership that bridges:
* Deep computational rigor (PhD-level Mechanical Engineering).
* Authentic mastery of Japanese Manufacturing Culture (TPS, Kaizen, Nemawashi).
* AI Knowledge Engineering (building semantic graphs for Industry 4.0).

## Integrated Knowledge Engineering (AI Advisor)
The application features a custom-built, locally integrated **Strategic AI Advisor** powered by the Gemini API. This LLM is specifically prompted with contextual regional data, TPS methodologies, and local institutional updates (like Alamo ATI) to provide real-time strategic insights into supply chain bottlenecks.
